Overview & Intraoperative Team Roles

Intraoperative nursing encompasses patient care during the surgical procedure, from induction of anesthesia to emergence. Key roles: circulating nurse (manages environment, documentation, unsterile supplies), scrub nurse (maintains sterile field, passes instruments), surgical first assistant. Primary goals: prevent infection, maintain safety, ensure hemostasis, and teamwork.

CRITICAL: Communication breakdown is the leading cause of retained surgical items and wrong-site surgery. Standardized checklist reduces errors by 50%.
Circulating Nurse
• Coordinates OR activities, documents intraop events.
• Provides supplies, monitors environmental safety.
• Performs "time out" verification, counts with scrub nurse.
• Advocates for patient during anesthesia.
Scrub Nurse / Technologist
• Sets up sterile instruments and drapes.
• Anticipates surgeon’s needs, passes instruments.
• Maintains integrity of sterile field, handles specimens.
• Assists in counting sponges, needles, instruments.
Key Statistics (AORN): Retained surgical items occur in 1 per 5,500 surgeries; 88% are sponges. Proper counts and X‑ray when discrepancy occurs reduce risk by 95%.

Key Intraoperative Nursing Responsibilities

Aseptic Technique
• Sterile gown/glove application, draping.
• One-inch border rule, only sterile-to-sterile contact.
• Never turn back to sterile field.
Positioning & Safety
• Prevent nerve damage (ulnar, brachial plexus, peroneal).
• Use padding, safety straps, avoid pressure points.
• Trendelenburg, lithotomy, prone – each has specific risks.
Surgical Counts
• Sponges, sharps, instruments counted three times (initial, before closure, after skin closure).
• Discrepancy → immediate X‑ray, notify surgeon.
• Never bypass count.
Preventing hypothermia: Forced‑air warmer, warmed IV fluids, increase ambient temperature, cover exposed areas. Hypothermia increases SSI risk by 300%.

Intraoperative Complications & Early Recognition

🚨 Malignant Hyperthermia (MH)
• Triggered by succinylcholine/volatile anesthetics.
• Early signs: masseter spasm, tachycardia, hypercapnia, rigidity, hyperthermia.
• Immediate dantrolene (2.5 mg/kg IV), stop triggers, hyperventilate, cold IV fluids.
⚠️ Hemorrhage / Hypovolemia
• Signs: tachycardia, hypotension, decreased urine output, pale field.
• Nursing: large bore IV, fluid resuscitation, blood products, communicate with surgeon.
❄️ Hypothermia (core <36°C)
→ coagulopathy, SSI, prolonged recovery. Forced-air warmer + warmed fluids mandatory.
🧠 Nerve Injury Prevention
• Brachial plexus: arms ≤90° abduction, padding. Peroneal: avoid lithotomy stirrup pressure. Document baseline neuro status.
MH EMERGENCY DRILL: Call for dantrolene, change anesthesia circuit, hyperventilate with 100% O₂, administer cold saline, monitor for arrhythmias.

Safety & Aseptic Management

Universal Protocol (Time Out)
• Verify patient identity, procedure, site, consent, implants, allergies, equipment availability.
• Performed immediately before incision with entire team.
• Mandatory by Joint Commission.
Sterile Technique Violations
• Turn away from field, moisture wicking, below waist is unsterile.
• Re‑gown/reglove if contamination occurs.
• Double gloving recommended for high‑risk cases.
Antibiotic prophylaxis (AORN): Administer within 60 minutes before incision (vancomycin 120 min). Redose at 2 half‑lives (e.g., cefazolin every 4 hours).

Nursing Care & Surgical Counts

  • Documentation: Intraoperative record: vital signs q5‑15min, fluids, medications, implants, counts, positioning, skin assessment.
  • Sponge/Sharps Count Protocol: Baseline count before incision → first closure count → final closure count → skin closure. All counts must match; X‑ray if mismatch before leaving OR.
  • Specimen Management: Label correctly in surgeon’s presence, use sterile container, verify with surgeon, send to lab with requisition immediately.
  • Preventing Pressure Injury: Use gel pads, reposition if possible, assess high‑risk areas (occiput, sacrum, heels).
Mnemonic “SPONGE” for counts: Separate counts, Perform X‑ray if needed, Observe field, Notify team, Give final verification, End with correct count.

SSI Prevention & Safety Checklist

🛡️ Preoperative bundle
• Shower with antiseptic night before, avoid razors (clippers only).
• Glucose control (<180 mg/dL), smoking cessation, decolonization if MRSA carrier.
📋 Intraoperative measures
• Maintain normothermia (36‑38°C), administer appropriate antibiotics, use sterile drapes, minimize OR traffic.
• Perform surgical hand antisepsis for 2‑5 minutes.
🚪 Postoperative closure
• Use sterile dressing, keep intact for 24‑48h, monitor for infection signs (erythema, purulent drainage).
📝 WHO Checklist Core
Sign In, Time Out, Sign Out. Reduces mortality and complications by >30%.
Key insight: Team huddle before incision (“Time Out”) is the last line of defense against wrong‑site surgery.

Flashcards & Mnemonics

Q: Role of circulating nurse?
Coordinates OR, documents, maintains safety, assists team, performs Time Out.
Q: When is surgical Time Out performed?
Immediately before incision (after draping).
Q: Signs of malignant hyperthermia?
Masseter spasm, tachycardia, hypercapnia, rigidity, hyperthermia.
Q: First-line drug for MH?
Dantrolene sodium (2.5 mg/kg IV). Repeat as needed.
Q: How many surgical counts required?
3 times: initial, before wound closure, after skin closure.
Q: Best position to prevent brachial plexus injury?
Arms abducted ≤90°, supine with padded arm boards.
